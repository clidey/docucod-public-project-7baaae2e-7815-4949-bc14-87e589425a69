## Installation Guide for Dark Reader

Dark Reader enhances your browsing experience by applying a customizable dark theme to websites, reducing eye strain and improving readability. This guide walks you through installing Dark Reader on your preferred browser, enabling you to activate dark mode quickly and reliably.

## Before You Begin

Ensure your browsing environment supports Dark Reader by confirming the following prerequisites:

- Use a supported browser:
  - Google Chrome or any Chromium-based browser (e.g., Microsoft Edge, Brave)
  - Mozilla Firefox
- Have the latest stable version of your browser installed to guarantee compatibility.
- Stable internet connection to download the extension from the official store.
- Access to your browser’s extension or add-on marketplace.

For detailed compatibility notes and system recommendations, see the [Prerequisites & System Requirements](/getting-started/quick-setup/system-prerequisites) page.

## Installing Dark Reader

Installing Dark Reader is straightforward and designed to complete within minutes. This section covers installation on Chrome, Firefox, and other Chromium-based browsers.

<Steps>
<Step title="Install on Google Chrome or Chromium-based Browsers">
1. Open the Chrome Web Store at https://chrome.google.com/webstore/category/extensions
2. Search for 'Dark Reader'.
3. Click **Add to Chrome**.
4. Confirm by clicking **Add extension** in the popup.

You should see the Dark Reader icon appear in your browser’s toolbar, indicating successful installation.
</Step>
<Step title="Install on Mozilla Firefox">
1. Visit the Firefox Add-ons site at https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/
2. Search for 'Dark Reader'.
3. Click **Add to Firefox**.
4. Confirm any permissions and click **Add**.

Dark Reader will now appear in your browser's extensions bar.
</Step>
<Step title="Verify the Installation">
After installation, open any website you visit regularly. Activate Dark Reader by clicking the extension icon and toggling the switch to "On". The page should immediately switch to dark mode.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Alternative Installation Methods

If you prefer, you can install Dark Reader manually by downloading the source from the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/darkreader/darkreader) and loading it as an unpacked extension in developer mode, but the above method is recommended for all users.

## Configuring Dark Reader after Installation

Dark Reader works out of the box, but you may want to configure settings to suit your preferences. Key configuration options include brightness, contrast, sepia tone, and font settings.

To access configuration:

1. Click the Dark Reader extension icon in your browser toolbar.
2. Adjust settings as desired using the sliders and toggles.
3. Customize per-site settings by enabling or disabling Dark Reader for specific domains.

Here is a minimal example for enabling Dark Reader with default settings on all websites:

```json
{
  "mode": "dark",
  "brightness": 100,
  "contrast": 90,
  "grayscale": 0,
  "sepia": 0,
  "fontFamily": null
}
```

Detailed configuration options and their explanations can be found on the [First-Time Configuration](/getting-started/quick-setup/configuration-basics) page.

## Running Dark Reader for the First Time

Activating Dark Reader transforms bright websites into comfortable, dark-themed pages instantly. Follow these steps to verify a successful first run:

<Steps>
<Step title="Open a Website">
Navigate to any frequently used website, such as https://example.com.
</Step>
<Step title="Activate Dark Reader">
Click the Dark Reader icon in your browser.
Toggle the switch to the "On" position. The page should immediately shift to dark mode.
</Step>
<Step title="Check Appearance">
Confirm if the website colors have changed appropriately, improving readability without affecting content visibility.
</Step>
</Steps>

If the page does not change:

- Ensure the extension is enabled in your browser's extensions manager.
- Try refreshing the web page.
